5  Thana-Level Population Distribution and Age Group Analysis

5.1 Overview

This document presents a reproducible analysis workflow for examining population records by Thana, City Corporation classification, and Age Group. The experiment focuses on cleaning location labels, separating records into areas inside and outside the City Corporation, summarizing population counts, and calculating percentage distributions.

The analysis uses a cleaned Excel dataset loaded from an environment variable. The workflow is designed to support transparent reporting, verification, and future replication without hard-coding the input file path.

5.2 Objectives

The main objectives of this experiment are to:

  • Load the cleaned dataset from a configured environment path.
  • Remove records from Thanas that are outside the intended analysis scope.
  • Standardize inconsistent or alternative Thana names through mapping.
  • Classify Thanas into City Corporation and non-City Corporation groups.
  • Create an age-based grouping using a threshold of 30 years.
  • Produce total population counts and Thana-wise summaries.
  • Calculate percentage distributions for City Corporation, outside City Corporation, and overall dataset shares.
  • Run a verification check to confirm whether all records are accounted for in the final grouping.

5.21 Global Population Share and Verification

The final section calculates the City Corporation share of the total dataset and the individual outside-City-Corporation Thana shares. These percentages are then summed to verify whether the classification accounts for the entire filtered dataset.

If the combined percentage does not equal 100%, the output warns that some records may still contain missing, unmapped, or unclassified Thana values.

# Ensure we have the global total
total_population = len(df)

print("       GLOBAL POPULATION SHARE BREAKDOWN          ")


# 1. Percentage of records in CC based on total population
cc_pct_global = (len(df[df["Thana"].isin(thanas_in_cc)]) / total_population) * 100
print(f"Total City Corporation (CC) Share: {cc_pct_global:.2f}%\n")

print("--------------------------------------------------")
print("Individual Thana Shares (Outside CC):")
# 2. Percentage of records in each thana outside CC based on total population
outside_thana_pct_global = (df[df["Thana"].isin(thanas_outside_cc)]["Thana"].value_counts() / total_population) * 100
print(outside_thana_pct_global.round(2).to_string())


print("                VERIFICATION CHECK                ")


# Summing CC total + individual outside thana percentages
grand_total_percentage = cc_pct_global + outside_thana_pct_global.sum()

## Overview

This document presents a reproducible analysis workflow for examining population records by **Thana**, **City Corporation classification**, and **Age Group**. The experiment focuses on cleaning location labels, separating records into areas inside and outside the City Corporation, summarizing population counts, and calculating percentage distributions.

The analysis uses a cleaned Excel dataset loaded from an environment variable. The workflow is designed to support transparent reporting, verification, and future replication without hard-coding the input file path.

## Objectives

The main objectives of this experiment are to:

- Load the cleaned dataset from a configured environment path.
- Remove records from Thanas that are outside the intended analysis scope.
- Standardize inconsistent or alternative Thana names through mapping.
- Classify Thanas into City Corporation and non-City Corporation groups.
- Create an age-based grouping using a threshold of 30 years.
- Produce total population counts and Thana-wise summaries.
- Calculate percentage distributions for City Corporation, outside City Corporation, and overall dataset shares.
- Run a verification check to confirm whether all records are accounted for in the final grouping.

## Standardizing Thana Names

Some records contain alternative spellings, informal names, administrative variants, or localized labels for the same Thana. A mapping dictionary is used to standardize these names into the classification labels used in the analysis.

This step is important because inconsistent naming can split the same geographic area into multiple categories and distort count-based summaries.


## Global Population Share and Verification

The final section calculates the City Corporation share of the total dataset and the individual outside-City-Corporation Thana shares. These percentages are then summed to verify whether the classification accounts for the entire filtered dataset.

If the combined percentage does not equal 100%, the output warns that some records may still contain missing, unmapped, or unclassified Thana values.

```{python}
# Ensure we have the global total
total_population = len(df)

print("       GLOBAL POPULATION SHARE BREAKDOWN          ")


# 1. Percentage of records in CC based on total population
cc_pct_global = (len(df[df["Thana"].isin(thanas_in_cc)]) / total_population) * 100
print(f"Total City Corporation (CC) Share: {cc_pct_global:.2f}%\n")

print("--------------------------------------------------")
print("Individual Thana Shares (Outside CC):")
# 2. Percentage of records in each thana outside CC based on total population
outside_thana_pct_global = (df[df["Thana"].isin(thanas_outside_cc)]["Thana"].value_counts() / total_population) * 100
print(outside_thana_pct_global.round(2).to_string())


print("                VERIFICATION CHECK                ")


# Summing CC total + individual outside thana percentages
grand_total_percentage = cc_pct_global + outside_thana_pct_global.sum()
print(f"Combined Sum of Percentages: {grand_total_percentage:.2f}%")

if round(grand_total_percentage, 2) == 100.00:
    print(" Check Passed: Perfect 100%! All data is accounted for.")
else:
    print("⚠️ Check Warning: Does not equal 100%. You may have missing (NaN) values or unmapped Thanas in your raw data.")
```

## Interpretation Guide

Use the generated outputs as follows:

- **Total Population** shows the number of valid records after filtering and removing missing Thana values.
- **Total records in CC** shows how many records fall inside the City Corporation classification.
- **Outside CC Thana counts** show how the non-City-Corporation records are distributed geographically.
- **Age Group summaries** show whether records are concentrated in the `<=30` or `>30` group.
- **Within-group percentages** explain internal composition, such as the age distribution inside a specific Thana.
- **Global percentages** show how much each group contributes to the entire filtered dataset.
- **Verification check** confirms whether all standardized Thana records are covered by either the City Corporation list or the outside City Corporation list.
